Ghib.li Human Language Analysis

Ghib.li provides two state-of-the-art Human Language Analysis tools to perform syntactic and semantic analysis.

Syntactic analysis is the process of analyzing human language into its constituents, for example, a sentence's subject and objects. Syntactic analysis can be used to extract the most relevant words of a sentence, which can save time and resources in the preparation of reports and dashboard.

Semantic analysis aims at finding the meaning of a sentence words, is more complex and less understood than syntactic analysis. State-of-the-art tools like Thomson Reuters' OpenCalais annotate text with a wealth of additional information, including topic, social tags and entities (e.g., companies, persons and countries). Ghib.li integrates OpenCalais in its services (a free OpenCalais service key is required) to streamline processes and facilitate the development of full-featured text processing tools.

Go to the Smart Text Classification Wiki and the Human Text Analysis Wiki for more details, or email info@ghib.li
